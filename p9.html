About the Author

Dr Xand van Tulleken is well known for presenting popular TV programmes with his identical twin brother Dr Chris van Tulleken. He has presented numerous shows for the BBC and Channel 4 including the BAFTA-winning Operation Ouch, Blow Your Mind, Horizon, Secret Life of Twins and Medicine Men Gone Wild. He has also contributed medical analysis on CNN, BBC1 and 2, and Al Jazeera among others. As a junior doctor he worked in the genocide in Darfur and this inspired a passion for the politics and medicine of conflict zones which lead to his academic appointment in the US. He now works between New York, Harvard and various humanitarian crises all over the world.

Book Surfer 3.0 out of 5 stars Light on explanation, strong on recipes Reviewed in the United Kingdom on August 22, 2017 Verified Purchase I can't fault the logic behind this 'diet', but after telling the reader that he's fed up with scientists not explaining things properly, he goes on to just present a very light version of his science. He tries to keep it simple, saying basically that you need to eat fewer calories than you burn, but doesn't go much into which foods are best and why. But it's clear he has views on this because there are certain things prioritised in the recipes and others completely cut out. For example he cuts out gluten almost entirely from all the recipes, but never mentions anything about this in the explanatory section. There bulk of the book contains recipes. These are good, and it's worth getting the hard copy for them I think, as they're not so easy to use on a kindle. I would probably not have bought it if I had known it was mostly recipes, but that's because I've been looking at nutrition for a while so very little of what he said was new to me. However if I was a first time dieter, or somebody who had failed with lots of fad diets, it'd be worth it, because what he says is simple and makes a lot of sense.
